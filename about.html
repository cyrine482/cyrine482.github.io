Hello! Welcome to my world

Complex systems • Epidemiology • Climate & Health

I am a researcher driven by curiosity about how living systems evolve, interact, and sometimes fail under environmental and social pressures. My work sits at the intersection of mathematics, epidemiology, ecology, and climate and health, where I use modeling and computational approaches to explore complex questions that rarely fit neatly into a single discipline.

I am particularly fascinated by systems in motion: the spread of infectious diseases, the dynamics of vector populations, the influence of climate on health, and the hidden patterns that emerge from biological and ecological interactions. Much of my work relies on theoretical and agent-based models, not only to predict outcomes, but also to better understand the mechanisms and underlying processes that generate them.

Currently, I work on climate and health research initiatives within the Pasteur Network, contributing to interdisciplinary projects that connect public health, environmental change, and scientific collaboration across countries and disciplines.

Beyond research, I am deeply interested in science diplomacy, scientific communication, and the role of interdisciplinary thinking in addressing global challenges. I enjoy translating complex ideas into clear narratives, learning across fields, and approaching science as both an analytical and creative process.

At the core of everything I do is a simple motivation: understanding complexity in order to make systems—whether ecological, scientific, or human—more resilient and more connected.
